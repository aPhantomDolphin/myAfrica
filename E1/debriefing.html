Debriefing

1. What is the general aim of this research?
The experiment you participated in today is part of a study investigating the impact of different adaptive spacing algorithms on recall. An adaptive spacing algorithm is an algorithm that changes the order items are shown to you based on your performance during the session. We are interested in whether your memory will be better using one of these algorithms compared to the others.
2. Is this correlational or experimental research?
The study you participated in represents experimental research. The independent variable we manipulated was the algorithm used to schedule your items and the dependent variable we are interested in is your performance on a final test. We predict that practicing the adaptive algorithms will not have significantly different performance from each other.
4. Where can I learn more about this type of research?
If you are interested in learning more about adaptive spacing algorithms, here are two relevant references:
  • Mettler, E., Massey, C. M., & Kellman, P. J. (2016). A comparison of adaptive and fixed schedules of practice. Journal of Experimental Psychology: General, 145(7), 897–917.
  • Tabibian, B., Upadhyay, U., De, A., Zarezade, A., Schölkopf, B., & Gomez-Rodriguez, M. (2019). Enhancing human learning via spaced repetition optimization. Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences, 116(10), 3988–3993.
